CITY HALL

  • Description
  • Contact
  • Category

CITY HALL (also known as Magi-strate, Rathaus, Varoshaz) which was built in 1895, is situated at the Square of Liberty and represents a monumental NeoRenaissance building. Back in 1855, they had a competition at which all the builders of the Austrian Empire could compete. The job was given to the so called ’’ baumeister’’(in German-construction worker) Georg Molnar. Finally, he fulfilled his mission and finished the building containing four domes, one at each corner, and a tower at the top of it, with a balcony. On the top of the tower, there used to be a bell serving as fire alarm. Citizens of Novi Sad gave this bell a name: Matilda. In wartime, the bell was remelted, but it has not been forgotten. The most noticeable elements of the facade are allegoric statues made by Julije Anika, a sculptor from Novi Sad. Sculptures made of plaster represent Greek mythology, human activities and highly moral presentations as a rule. Today, city hall is the seat of the Mayor and some appropriate services. Inside, there is a ceremonial hall which was decorated by a painter Pavle Ruzicka. He made certain medallions representing the symbols of agricultural, tradesmen and craftsmen class.

DUNAVSKI PARK

  • Description
  • Contact
  • Category

DUNAVSKI PARK lies alongside the Dunavska Street which it was named after. In the beginning of the 19th century at this place was a marshy terrain. They started drying it up and arranging it, and later backfilling with soil in order to construct a hotel that would be called ‘’Kodengleskekraljice’’ (At the English Queen). Later, that building became the seat of the Court and after that the Museum of Vojvodina. DunavskiPark is a protected natural area and park. Visitors can find a few busts and sculptures in the park, too; pets: BrankoRadicevic and Miroslav Antic, and both a poet and a painter DjuraJaksic, a sculpture of a nymph holding the horn of plenty and a monument of venerable Sergei Radonieski. Among 750 trees in the park, you can find: sycamore, poplar, hazel, birch, hackberry, horse chestnut and others, but the main reason for protection is a large oak tree. What makes this park special is a lake in the middle of it.And not only the lake but its inhabitants: a pair of swans called Isa and Bisa. In this beautiful park a lot of interesting shows, concerts and performances take place.

THE STRAND

  • Description
  • Contact
  • Category

THE STRAND is the most popular city beach. It was open in 1911. Today, it is long 700 metres and it can be said that it is a small town within the city with its catering and playgrounds, especially for beach volley and tennis played with head, which is said that was invented here. The strand is a favourite meeting place of the youngest. The older, also adore visiting this place which has all the necessary infrastructural facilities. During the summer, the strand is one of the most visited places in the city because of the competitions, concerts and festivals which are held there.

PETROVARADIN FORTRESS

  • Description
  • Contact
  • Category

PETROVARADIN FORTRESS – sometimes called ‘’Gibraltar on the Danube had been built between 1659 and 1780. It takes 112 acres, and is divided into the upper and a lower town. On the upper town, there are the bastions: Lower Ludwig’s bastion, Upper Ludwig’s bastion, Innocentie’s, Maria Theresa’s, St. Leopold’s and Tsar Joseph’s. Access is possible through the gates of: Ludwig, Leopold, Molinary, Karlo VI and Court gate. Some authentic places are still preserved like: Officer’s pavilion, Leopold powder magazine, Proviant magazine, Arsenal, the long barrack, the clock tower and a great wartime well. This whole complex was created according to the concept of marquis Sebastian Voban (1633-1707). Among the builders, the most prominent place is taken by an Italian count Alois Luigi Ferdinando Marsili (1659-1730). He was a versatile personality, highly educated writer, historian, cartographer, archeologist, architect, builder and soldier. He wrote a book on the Danube, published in The Hague in 1726 in which he shares some facts about Petrovaradin and Cenej (suburban part of Novi Sad). His legacy is held in Marsili Museum in Bologna, which exists since 1930.
